The Mentor-Ask Prompt Pack

Copy a prompt, swap the [brackets], paste it into Copilot or any AI. Keep the warmth — lose the over-explaining.

We over-articulate to prove ourselves. A good prompt helps you land the message with less — one clear ask, in half the words. Here are five to start with.
Make the ask

1 · Ask someone at your company

For a colleague or leader you admire and want to learn from
You're my writing assistant. Help me write a warm, confident message (4 sentences max) to [name], a [role] at my company I genuinely admire. I'd like them to mentor me on [skill or area]. Say specifically why I'm asking them, make one clear ask for a recurring 30-minute chat, and cut any over-apologizing — no "sorry to bother you," no "if it's not too much trouble."

2 · Ask someone on LinkedIn

For someone who doesn't know you yet
Help me write a short LinkedIn message (3–4 sentences) to [name], who doesn't know me. I admire their work on [topic]. I want to ask for a 15-minute virtual coffee — not a job, just to learn how they think about [topic]. Make it genuine, specific, low-pressure, and easy to say yes to. No flattery overload.
Land it with less

3 · Tighten a message I already wrote

When your draft feels too long or too apologetic
Here's a message I drafted. Keep my warmth and my point, but cut it to about half the length and remove the hedging — words like "just," "maybe," "sorry," "I was wondering if." End with one clear ask. Here's my draft: [paste your message]
Show up prepared

4 · Prep for a 15-minute coffee

So you walk in ready and leave with a next step
I have a 15-minute virtual coffee with [name], who is a [role]. Help me prepare: 3 thoughtful questions to ask them, 1 thing I could offer or follow up on, and a natural way to close with "based on our conversation, who else should I get to know?" Keep it conversational, not scripted.

5 · Follow up and say thank you

The part most people skip — and it's how you stay memorable
Write a brief thank-you note (3 sentences) to [name] after our [15-minute coffee / mentoring chat]. Mention one specific thing I took away, confirm the next step if we agreed on one, and keep it warm and genuine.
See the difference
Before"So sorry to bother you — I know you're incredibly busy. I was just wondering if maybe you might have a little time to chat and give me some advice? Totally fine if not!"
After"I really admire how you lead the architecture reviews. I'm growing in that area and would value your guidance. Could we set a recurring 30-minute chat once a month?"

Same warmth. Half the words. One clear ask.
